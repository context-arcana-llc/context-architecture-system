# Decision Record — Shift to Direct-Trade Sourcing

**Date:** 2025-09-04
**Status:** Accepted

---

## Context

Since launch we have sourced all green coffee through a regional importer. This simplified procurement but removed visibility into origin pricing and limited the stories we could tell customers about the farms we work with.

Two of our wholesale accounts — Oakcliff Bistro and The Larder — have asked directly about farm relationships. The Saturday market booth also draws more questions about sourcing than we anticipated.

---

## Decision

Move to direct-trade sourcing for at least two of our four current origins by Q1 2026. Maintain the importer relationship for the remaining origins while we build direct contacts.

---

## Reasoning

- Direct-trade pricing should improve margin at scale, though the first season involves more overhead
- Farm stories are a differentiator we cannot buy through an importer
- The 12kg drum upgrade (see `2026-03_drum-roaster-upgrade.md`) increases our ability to commit to minimum volumes that make direct relationships viable

---

## Consequences

- Mara to attend two origin trips in 2025 Q4 (Ethiopia and Colombia)
- Need to add a "sourcing" section to `context/01_identity/` once relationships are confirmed
- Importer contract reviewed annually — no lock-in beyond current season
