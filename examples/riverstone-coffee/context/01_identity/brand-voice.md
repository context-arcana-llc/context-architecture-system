# Brand Voice

## Core principles

1. **Sensory, not academic.** Describe the coffee in terms a customer can taste, not in terms a Q-grader uses. "Cocoa, blackcurrant, dried apricot" — not "SCA score 87.5."
2. **Regional, not global.** Reference local context (Hollingford, the mill, the river) over abstract "craft" language.
3. **Direct, not effusive.** We don't say "exquisite" or "elevated." We say what it is.
4. **Honest about scale.** We're small. We say so.

---

## Tone signature phrases

- "Roasted in small batches"
- "Single-origin, direct trade"
- "From the mill in Hollingford"
- "Pickup at the Saturday market"

---

## Avoid

- "Premium" / "luxury" / "exclusive" — we're approachable, not aspirational
- "Curated" — used to death
- "Artisanal" — implies precious; we're matter-of-fact
- Coffee jargon (Q-score, washed/natural without explanation) in customer-facing copy
- Generic origin claims ("from the highlands of...")
- Anything that sounds like a marketing email from 2014

---

## Do / Don't

| Don't | Do |
|---|---|
| "Our exquisite, curated single-origin offerings" | "This month: a washed Colombian from Huila, roasted light." |
| "Premium subscription experience" | "Two bags every four weeks, delivered Tuesday." |
| "Hand-crafted with passion" | "Roasted Tuesdays. Bagged Wednesdays. Shipped Thursdays." |
| "Discover the elevated taste of artisanal coffee" | "Bag opens. Beans smell good. The rest you can figure out." |
| "Award-winning, world-class roasts" | "We've been roasting in Hollingford since 2023." |

---

## When in doubt

If a sentence would sound out of place spoken aloud at the Saturday market booth, rewrite it.
